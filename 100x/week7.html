Week 7

Assessment & Learning Models

Learning Model: Experiential Learning

Learning Scenario

In this scenario, students will learn HTML, the fundamental language used to create web pages, through hands-on experience and real-world application. The learning activity involves creating a basic webpage from scratch using HTML tags.

Formative Assessment

Create a checklist to evaluate students' understanding and application of HTML tags. The checklist includes items such as the correct usage of basic HTML tags, organization of content, and proper indentation.

Authentic Assessment

Students will create a webpage for a community event. This assessment ties to experiential learning, providing students with a real-world context to apply their HTML skills. Students would need to communicate with the organization to gather requirements, consider audience needs, and design a webpage that effectively conveys relevant information. This assessment encourages student-centered learning as students take ownership of their projects and have a tangible impact on their community through their work.

Connection to Learning Theory

Constructivism

In this scenario, students explore HTM and collaborate with peers to co-construct knowledge and solve authentic problems. The formative and authentic assessment allows students to apply their HTML knowledge, collaboration, and critical thinking skills in real-world contexts.
