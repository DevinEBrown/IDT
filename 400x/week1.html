Minicourse Features & LMS Declaration

Mini-Course/Course Title

Building User-Centric Websites

Course Overview

Building User-Centric Websites course is designed to introduce learners to the principles and practices of web design. This Mini-Course aims to introduce responsive web design, equipping learners with the skills to create websites that adapt to different screens and devices.

View Course

Mini-Course Features

Video Lectures/Multimedia Presenations: Pre-recorded videos that explain key concepts of web design, including responsive design principles and best practices.

Interactive Quizzes: Assessments that test learners' understanding of the material covered in the video lectures, providing immediate feedback.

Hands-On Projects: Practical assignments that require learners to apply responsive web design techniques to create their websites.

Discussions: Online forums where learners can discuss challenges, share ideas, and collaborate on projects, fostering a sense of community.

Live Q&A Sessions: Live webinars or virtual classrooms where learners can ask questions and receive guidance from instructors in real-time.

LMS Choice

Google Classroom is my preferred learning management system (LMS) for grades 9-12 due to its user-friendly interface and seamless integration with Google Workspace tools. It offers a variety of features that support my minicourse on Introduction to Web Design Layout, such as the ability to create assignments and quizzes, facilitate discussions through the class stream and Question feature, and provide timely feedback to students. Additionally, Google Classroom allows for easy organization of course materials, including videos, articles, and presentations, which are essential for delivering the content of the minicourse effectively. Its integration with Google Drive also enables students to collaborate on projects and access resources from anywhere, fostering a more engaging and dynamic learning experience.

Support Resources

For Google Classroom, there are several support resources available to help you build your minicourse. The first is the official Google Classroom Help Center, which provides detailed guides, tutorials, and troubleshooting tips. Another valuable resource is YouTube, where you can find a wealth of video tutorials and demonstrations on how to use Google Classroom. Additionally, Google for Education's official blog often publishes articles and updates related to Google Classroom, providing insights and best practices for educators.
