Learning Theory/Models & Opening Presentation

Learning Theories

In designing the minicourse Building User-Centric Websites, I found both Constructivism and Connectivism influential. Constructivism guided my decisions regarding content, activities, and assessments by emphasizing the importance of active learning and meaningful engagement. For example, I included hands-on activities like creating color schemes and using design tools like Figma, allowing students to construct their understanding through practical experience.

Connectivism influenced the design by highlighting the role of technology in learning and the importance of networked learning. I incorporated discussion forums and encouraged students to explore online resources, promoting a more connected learning experience. Additionally, assessments were designed to evaluate not just individual knowledge but also the ability to navigate and leverage online resources effectively.

These theories enhanced the learning experience by making it more interactive, relevant, and connected. For example, by using Figma, students not only learned about design principles but also gained practical skills that are directly applicable in the field. The use of online resources and discussion forums expanded their learning beyond the classroom, aligning with Connectivist principles of learning in a digital age.

Overall, Constructivist and Connectivist principles made the minicourse more engaging, practical, and reflective of real-world learning experiences in web design.

Learning Models

I primarily followed the Successive Approximation Model (SAM). This model's iterative nature influenced the course's structure, content, and delivery methods by emphasizing rapid prototyping and continuous refinement. It guided me to develop the course in smaller increments, allowing for more frequent feedback and adjustments.

The SAM model's specific phases, such as the preparation, design, and development, helped me structure the course development process. For example, during the preparation phase, I thoroughly analyzed learners' needs and the course objectives. During the design phase, I created detailed outlines and storyboards. In the development phase, I created engaging multimedia content and interactive activities.

The most significant challenge I encountered was balancing the iterative nature of SAM with the limited time and resources available. Iterative development requires frequent revisions, which could be time-consuming. However, the benefit was that it allowed for a more refined final product that better met the needs of the learners.

Overall, the SAM model facilitated meeting learning objectives by ensuring the course content was continually refined based on feedback. It also helped address the needs of learners by allowing for flexibility and adaptation throughout the development process.
