Typography Overview

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

  1. Define typography and its role in web design.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (1,3)
  2. Categorize typefaces into major classifications (serif, sans-serif, script, etc.) and explain the characteristics that define each category.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (1)
  3. Evaluate best practices of typography in responsive web design.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (3,4)

Course Invite

Discussion Prompt 1

Discuss a website where typography significantly conveys the message. How does the choice of typeface, font size, spacing, and alignment contribute to the overall effectiveness of the communication? Provide examples to support your argument.

Discussion Prompt 2

Typography can evoke different emotions and convey different meanings based on its use. Choose a typeface and describe how it might be perceived in various contexts (e.g., formal, casual, playful, serious). How can designers leverage typography to enhance the user experience on a website?

Discuussion Rubrics

Assignment Prompt

Design a webpage layout for a fictitious business, focusing on typography. Select a theme or concept for the business (e.g., a bakery, a tech startup, a travel agency) and choose typefaces, font sizes, spacing, and alignment that align with the brand's identity and target audience. Provide a rationale for your typography choices and how they contribute to the overall design and user experience.

Reflections

Creating the discussion and assignment rubrics was valuable in clarifying the expectations and criteria for evaluating student work. The process helped me identify key aspects of student performance that are important for the learning objectives. Using these rubrics, I can assess student understanding and engagement more effectively, providing specific feedback to guide their learning. Additionally, the rubrics allow me to identify areas where students may struggle, which can inform future lesson planning and instructional design. Overall, these rubrics are a useful tool for ensuring consistency in evaluation and continuously improving future learners' learning experience.
