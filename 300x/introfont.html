Introduction to Typography

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will be able to:

  1. Define typography and its role in web design.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (1,3)
  2. Categorize typefaces into major classifications (serif, sans-serif, script, etc.) and explain the characteristics that define each category.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (1)
  3. Evaluate best practices of typography in responsive web design.
    aligns to course learning outcome (CLO) (3,4)

Lesson 1

Understanding Typography in Web Design

In this lesson, students will delve into the fundamental concepts of typography and explore its significance in web design. The lesson will cover the basic definition of typography and how it contributes to the overall aesthetics and functionality of websites.

Lesson 2

Typography Classifications

This lesson focuses on the major classifications of typefaces, namely serif, sans-serif, and script. Students will learn to categorize typefaces based on these classifications and develop an understanding of the characteristics that distinguish each category.

Lesson 3

Responsive Web Design and Typography

This lesson focuses on integrating typography within responsive web design. Students will learn about the challenges and opportunities typography presents in the context of responsive design.

Lesson 4

Typography and Accessibility

his lesson emphasizes the importance of accessibility in typography and color choices. Students will learn about designing for inclusivity and ensuring that text remains legible for users with varying visual abilities. Topics will include color contrast ratios, WCAG guidelines

Lesson 5

Typography in Web Design Projects

In the final lesson, students will apply their knowledge by evaluating typography in real-world web design projects. They will analyze existing websites, assess their typographic choices, and critique their effectiveness.
