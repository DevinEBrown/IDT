Understanding by Design (UbD)

Overview

Understanding by Design (UbD) is an educational framework and curriculum design approach developed by Grant Wiggins and Jay McTighe. It is a backward design process that starts with the end goals in mind and works backward to develop curriculum units, lessons, and assessments. The model consists of the following stages:

Identify Desired Results: to define what students need to know, understand, and be able to do by the end of the unit or course.

Determine Acceptable Evidence: the assessment planning of how students have achieved the desired learning goals. This can involve various assessment methods, such as tests, projects, presentations, or other evaluation forms.

Plan Learning Experiences: the designing of learning experiences and activities that will help students achieve established goals. These activities are intended to facilitate deep understanding and transfer of knowledge.

Implications For Instructional Design

In web design, instructional designers would start by outlining the learning outcomes. For instance, a goal could be mastering user interface design principles. Assessments should align with this objective, such as evaluating a student's ability to create an intuitive and user-friendly website. Instructional designers might incorporate hands-on projects to promote deep understanding, allowing students to design and build actual websites rather than learn theoretical concepts. Essential questions could revolve around user experience, asking how design choices impact user engagement and navigation.

This encourages critical thinking and exploration. Emphasis should be on applying design principles, not just rote memorization. Students could be tasked with redesigning an existing website to improve its usability. To cater to diverse learners, options for different project formats (e.g., responsive design, interactive elements) could be provided. Finally, reflection and feedback opportunities are crucial.

After completing a web design project, students could review their work, identify strengths and areas for improvement, and set goals for future projects. This approach creates purposeful, engaging learning experiences that foster deep understanding and practical application of web design principles.

Strengths and Limitations

Strengths of Understanding by Design (UbD) in higher education include promoting meaningful learning, setting clear objectives, aligning assessments, fostering critical thinking, and emphasizing knowledge transfer. However, limitations include time-intensive planning, potential overemphasis on assessments, challenges in accommodating diverse learning styles, resource requirements, content prioritization, and potential complexity if not implemented effectively.
