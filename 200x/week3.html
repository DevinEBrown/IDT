Dick and Carey Model

Overview

The Dick and Carey Model is an instructional design framework developed by Walter Dick and Lou Carey. It provides a systematic approach to designing effective and efficient instruction. The model consists of ten steps:

  1. Identify Instructional Goals: This step involves identifying the desired learning outcomes or objectives that the instruction should achieve.
  2. Conduct Instructional Analysis: The instructional designer analyzes the content to be taught, the learner characteristics, and the learning environment.
  3. Define Entry Behaviors: The instructional designer determines the existing knowledge, skills, and attitudes that learners should possess before beginning the instruction.
  4. Write Performance Objectives: Specific, measurable performance objectives are written based on the identified instructional goals.
  5. Develop Assessment Instruments: Assessment tools and techniques are created to evaluate the learners' attainment of the performance objectives. This step ensures that the assessment aligns with the objectives.
  6. Develop Instructional Strategy: The selection of appropriate instructional strategies, materials, and resources to facilitate learning.
  7. Develop and Select Instructional Materials: The actual instructional materials, such as textbooks, presentations, or multimedia, are created or chosen based on the chosen instructional strategy.
  8. Design and Conduct Formative Evaluation: Formative evaluation involves testing the instructional materials and methods with a small group of learners to gather feedback and make necessary adjustments before full-scale implementation.
  9. Revise Instruction: Based on the feedback obtained from the formative evaluation, the instructional materials and methods are revised and refined to improve their effectiveness.
  10. Conduct Summative Evaluation: To assess whether the objectives have been met and to gather data on the overall effectiveness of the instruction. This step provides insights for the future.

Implications For Instructional Design

The Dick and Carey Model, a systematic instructional design framework, has significant implications for K-12 education. Its emphasis on a learner-centered approach and systematic analysis of instructional needs aligns well with the diverse learning styles and needs in K-12 settings. The model's step-by-step process, from identifying instructional goals to assessment, provides a structured guide for creating effective learning experiences. In K-12, this model supports the development of a curriculum that caters to varied student abilities, ensuring a more inclusive and comprehensive education. Additionally, the focus on formative evaluation allows for ongoing feedback and refinement, fostering continuous improvement in teaching materials and strategies. By employing the Dick and Carey Model, K-12 educators can enhance instructional effectiveness, promote student engagement, and adapt to the dynamic nature of contemporary education.

Strengths and Limitations

The Dick and Carey Model provides a comprehensive framework for designing courses and instructional materials in higher education, ensuring that learning objectives are clearly defined and aligned with assessment methods. The model's structured nature may be perceived as rigid and may not always accommodate higher education settings' diverse and evolving needs, where adaptability and innovation are often valued.
